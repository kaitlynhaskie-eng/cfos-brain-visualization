## Overview

This interactive visualization highlights brain regions showing elevated neuronal activation following mild traumatic brain injury (mTBI) in a mouse model of post-traumatic headache.

Neuronal activity was captured using the **cFos-TRAP system**, an activity-dependent genetic labeling technique that permanently marks neurons active during a defined time window. This is slice 7 of 8 Female 8, Tamoxifen 75 mg/kg, was administered two days after injury to label neurons active during the **acute phase of post-traumatic headache**.

Regional neuronal activation was quantified by measuring **cFos-positive cell density**, normalized by tissue area (cells/mm²). The displayed coronal brain section was registered to the **Allen Mouse Brain Atlas** using the **Aligning Big Brain Atlases (ABBA)** framework to identify anatomical regions.

The visualization integrates quantitative ranking with spatial brain mapping, allowing users to explore which brain regions show the strongest neuronal activation.

Region Selection

The regions shown here were selected through a curated ranking process based on cFos-positive cell density within atlas-defined brain regions. After registration to the Allen Mouse Brain Atlas using the Aligning Big Brain Atlases (ABBA) framework, cFos-TRAP labeled neurons were quantified and normalized by tissue area. Candidate regions were first ranked by density, then reviewed to exclude regions whose apparent enrichment was driven by extremely small anatomical areas or very low cell counts, which can inflate density estimates. The final set highlights regions with both high relative activation and more interpretable biological representation in this brain section.

## Interactive Features

- Bar chart ranking brain regions by cFos cell density
- Brain atlas overlay highlighting anatomical regions
- Linked interactions between the plot and brain map
- Hover and click interactions reveal detailed region information
- Density-based color mapping across brain regions
