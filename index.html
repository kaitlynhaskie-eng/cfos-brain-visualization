About the Data
This visualization highlights brain regions showing the highest neuronal activation two days after mild traumatic brain injury (mTBI) in a mouse model of post-traumatic headache. Neuronal activation was mapped using the cFos-TRAP system, an activity-dependent genetic labeling method that permanently marks neurons active during a defined time window. Tamoxifen was administered two days after injury to capture neurons active during the acute phase of post-traumatic headache. Cell counts were normalized by tissue area to estimate regional cFos cell density. The displayed coronal brain section was registered to the Allen Mouse Brain Atlas using the Aligning Big Brain Atlases (ABBA) framework to identify anatomical regions.
